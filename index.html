Pet Rescue: Autonoumous Robot

My ENPH 253 team (Luca Jones, Harchetan Chohan, Taichi Kamei) designed and built an autonomous rescue robot capable of navigating a complex obstacle course, identifying and retrieving “pet” targets, and returning them safely to the home zone, all without human control. The brain of our robot was an **ESP32 microcontroller**, chosen for its power and verstaility. A custom state machine was built to manage the various modes of operation such as line following and pet detection. The navigation of the robot relies on infared LED's and phototransistors to detect the line of electrical that traces throughout the entire course. By reading which phototransistors are on or off, the robot can determine its relative position to the line and make adjustments using **PID control** to stay on course. To identify and rescue the pets our robot utilizes **2D time-of-flight sensors** to detect the presence of pet targets. Next to grab the pets our robot uses a custom a **planar 2-bar linkage arm** and a **4-bar claw** to pick up the pet and store it in the bucket that is on board the robot. The arm and claw were built using **3D printing**, **lazer cutting**, and **water-jet cutting**. To lift the bucket adn hook it to the ziplien we designed a **continous cascade mechansim** that uses a dc motor to wind a rope around a spool, lifting the bucket vertically.
